About the Journal

The Journal of Gateway to Sustainable Research (JGSR) is an international, peer-reviewed, Open Access journal dedicated to publishing high-quality research that advances sustainable development across engineering, technology, management, and interdisciplinary domains.

The journal aims to provide a global platform for researchers, academicians, industry professionals, and policymakers to disseminate innovative ideas and practical solutions that address environmental, technological, industrial, and societal sustainability challenges.

JGSR encourages original, well-structured, and scientifically rigorous manuscripts that promote responsible innovation and long-term sustainable growth.

Aim & Scope

The journal welcomes original research articles, review papers, case studies, and technical communications in (but not limited to) the following areas:

  • Electrical & Electronics Engineering
  • Mechanical Engineering
  • Civil Engineering
  • Electronics & Communication Engineering
  • Computer Science & Engineering
  • Management Studies
  • Sustainable Technologies
  • Interdisciplinary Research related to Sustainability

Primary Objective

The primary objective of JGSR is to encourage and publish research that contributes to:

  • Environmental sustainability
  • Green and clean technologies
  • Industrial sustainability and optimization
  • Energy efficiency and renewable systems
  • Smart infrastructure and resilient systems
  • Sustainable business and management practices
  • Societal well-being through technological advancement

The journal promotes interdisciplinary collaboration that bridges engineering, science, and management toward sustainable development goals.

Long-Term Digital Archives / Preservation Policy

The Journal of Gateway to Sustainable Research is committed to ensuring permanent accessibility and digital preservation of its scholarly content.

LOCKSS

LOCKSS (Lots of Copies Keep Stuff Safe) is a decentralized archiving network that preserves web-published scholarly materials and protects against data loss or platform failure.

CLOCKSS

CLOCKSS ensures long-term survival of scholarly content. If the journal ceases operation, archived content will be made publicly available under an open-access framework.

PKP Preservation Network (PKP PN)

PKP PN provides an additional secure and redundant layer of long-term digital archiving for journals using Open Journal Systems (OJS).

Archival Scope

  • Full-text articles (PDF/HTML)
  • Supplementary files
  • Metadata and DOI records
  • Editorial content

This multi-layered preservation strategy ensures secure, redundant, and sustainable protection of scholarly output published in JGSR.
